Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

Wardrobe Log is built to be private by design. This policy explains, in plain language, what the app does and does not do with your information.

The short version

  • The app does not collect, transmit, or sell your data.
  • There are no accounts, no logins, and no tracking.
  • There are no analytics tools and no third-party SDKs.
  • The developer has no access to anything you enter.

What the app stores

Everything you create in Wardrobe Log stays on your device. This includes the clothing items you log (name, price, category, store, date, notes, mood, and any photo you choose to attach), your monthly budget, your greeting name, and your appearance and accent-color preferences.

iCloud sync

If you are signed in to iCloud, your data syncs across your own Apple devices using Apple's CloudKit service. This data lives in your private iCloud account and is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy. The developer cannot see or access it. You can turn this off at any time in your device's iCloud settings.

Photos

You may optionally attach a photo to an item. Photos are chosen through the standard system photo picker, which only shares the specific images you select. A selected photo is stored alongside its item — on your device and, if enabled, in your private iCloud. Photos are never uploaded anywhere else.

Notifications

The app uses background notifications solely so iCloud can keep your data in sync between your devices. These are not marketing messages and contain no personal content.

Data collected by the developer

None. Wardrobe Log does not include analytics, advertising, crash reporting tied to your identity, or any other form of data collection.

Children's privacy

Wardrobe Log does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new "Last updated" date.
